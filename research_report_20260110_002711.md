Artificial intelligence (AI) is rapidly transforming the drug discovery pipeline by applying computational models and machine learning algorithms to simulate, predict, and optimize various stages of therapeutic agent development [1, 2, 8, 10]. AI leverages vast amounts of chemical, biological, and clinical data to learn patterns and perform tasks that typically require human intelligence [2, 4].

## Key Applications of AI in Drug Discovery

AI's utility spans multiple critical phases of drug discovery and development:

### 1. Therapeutic Target Discovery
AI is employed to identify and validate novel therapeutic targets, which are crucial for developing new treatments [9]. By analyzing complex biological data, AI can pinpoint specific molecules or pathways that could be modulated by drugs.

### 2. De Novo Drug Design
AI enables the design of entirely new drug molecules from scratch, a process known as *de novo* drug design. This involves generating novel chemical structures with desired properties and activity against specific targets [7].

### 3. Lead Optimization
Once initial drug candidates (leads) are identified, AI plays a significant role in optimizing their properties. This includes improving efficacy, reducing toxicity, and enhancing pharmacokinetic profiles [4, 6]. Computational methods such as machine learning, deep learning, and graph neural networks are utilized to refine lead compounds within the drug discovery pipeline [4].

### 4. Drug Repurposing
AI is unlocking hidden value in drug repurposing by quickly identifying new therapeutic uses for existing medicines [5]. This approach accelerates patient access to therapies and generates cost savings by leveraging drugs that have already undergone extensive safety testing [5].

### 5. Prediction of Combination Therapies
AI can predict effective combination therapies by analyzing existing drugs and their potential synergistic effects. This involves using computational models to identify promising combinations that might offer enhanced efficacy or reduced side effects [2].

### 6. Quantitative Modeling
AI is crucial for quantitative modeling in drug discovery and development. It applies computational models and machine learning algorithms to simulate and predict various biological and chemical phenomena, providing insights into drug behavior and interactions [2, 3]. This includes predicting how drugs will interact with biological systems and optimizing their properties throughout the development process [2].

## Benefits and Impact
The integration of AI into drug discovery significantly accelerates the overall process, leading to faster patient access to therapies and considerable cost savings [5]. By processing and learning from extensive datasets, AI enhances the efficiency and effectiveness of identifying, designing, and optimizing therapeutic agents [2, 4].
